The Culture of Norway



Language

The offical languages of Norway are Norwegian and Sámi, the language of the indigenous Sámi people. Norwegian has two main variants; Bokmål, which is spoken by 80% to 90% of the population, and Nynorsk, spoken by 10% to 15% of the population. Bokmål is influenced by Danish and has many loanwords, while Nynorsk is based on Norwegian dialects. English is also spoken by 90% of the population. Other common second or third languages include:

  • German
  • Spanish
  • Danish
  • Lithuanian
  • Polish
  • Arabic
  • Somali

Religion and Beliefs

There are many religions in Norway, but the two largest are Christianity and Islam. 18% of Norwegians are unaffiliated with a religon. The largest religons in Norway are:

  1. Lutheranism - 64%
  2. Catholicism - 3%
  3. Islam - 3%
  4. Secular Humanism - 2%

Other notable religions include Hinduism, Bhuddhism, and Norse Paganism. Some beliefs are not associated with a particular religion, such as the tale that trolls live in the mountains of Norway.


Holidays

Norway has 5 main public holidays. These are:

  • New Year's
  • Easter
  • May Day
  • Constituion Day
  • Christmas Day

Food

Norwegian cuisine differs from most others in Europe due to its stronger focus on fish and game. The national dish of Norway is Fårikål, a mix of mutton, cabbage, pepper, and wheat flour. Other popular dishes include:

  • Flatbrød
  • Lefse
  • Lutefisk
  • Smalahove
